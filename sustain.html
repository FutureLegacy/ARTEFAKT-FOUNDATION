Pillar 04 / 04 — Sustain — Materials Reuse · Funding Model

Nothing of value
gets thrown away.

Sustain is Artefakt's funding engine and its clearest philosophical statement. A working materials-reuse and electronics-recycling operation runs out of the gallery's back room in Calgary. Revenue funds the Fellowship, the Academy, and the archive. Nothing of value should be thrown away — materials, memories, communities, or truth.

SST · 01
Stewardship
What Gets Left

Stewardship,
not charity.

IT asset disposal from regional partners, estate-sale acquisitions, end-of-lease cleanouts. Materials triaged, restored, resold at fair value. Margins go directly to programming.

SST · 02 — Materials
Working Collection · 2023

Triage · Resale

Objects carry consequence.

A laser printer. A film camera. A Steelcase chair. A Dyson motor. Every object that passes through Sustain has a history — an office closed, an estate settled, a contract finished.

The operation treats each one with the same care documentary practice brings to its subjects: look closely, describe accurately, find the next right place for it.

SST · 03
The Funding Model
Revenue · Programming

Margins become
fellowships.

Every dollar of margin from Sustain is ring-fenced for programming. A reconditioned printer pays for a Fellow's stipend week. An office clearance funds a masterclass honorarium.

SST · 04 — In the Neighbourhood
4525 Monterey NW

Operations · Live

Work that
stays local.

Sustain is physical. It happens at 4525 Monterey Avenue NW in Calgary — in the same storage room that holds the archival prints. The operation supports local freight, local repair, local buyers.

It is the opposite of extractive. It is, plainly, a small business at the service of a cultural mission — and over time, a template that other documentary organizations can copy.
