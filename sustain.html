Pillar 04 / 04 Sustain Materials Reuse · Funding Model

Nothing of value gets thrown away.

Sustain is Artefakt's funding engine and its clearest philosophical statement. A working materials-reuse and electronics-recycling operation is being established at the gallery at 4525 Monterey Ave NW, Calgary. Revenue funds the Fellowship, the Learn, and the archive. The logic extends in both directions: nothing of value should be thrown away — materials, memories, communities, or truth.

SST · 01 What Gets Left
Stewardship

Stewardship, not charity.

Sustain is not a donation drop-off. It is a disciplined operation — IT asset disposal from regional partners, estate-sale acquisitions, end-of-lease cleanouts from property managers. The materials are triaged, restored where they can be, and resold through marketplace channels at fair value. Margins go directly to programming.

SST · 02 Objects & Consequence
Materials

Objects carry consequence.

A laser printer. A film camera. A Steelcase chair. A Dyson motor. Every object that passes through Sustain has a history — an office closed, an estate settled, a contract finished. The operation treats each one with the same care documentary practice brings to its subjects: look closely, describe accurately, find the next right place for it.

SST · 03 Revenue · Programming
The Funding Model

Margins become fellowships.

Every dollar of margin from Sustain is ring-fenced for programming. A reconditioned printer pays for a Fellow's stipend week. An office clearance funds a masterclass honorarium. The accounting is transparent, and the logic is deliberate: the work of documentary should not depend on the goodwill of funders alone. It should have its own legs.

SST · 04 Neighborhood Economy
In The Neighborhood

Work that stays local.

Sustain is physical. It happens at 4525 Monterey Avenue NW in Calgary — in the same storage room that holds the archival prints. The operation supports local freight, local repair, local buyers. It is the opposite of extractive. It is, plainly, a small business at the service of a cultural mission — and over time, a template that other documentary organizations can copy.
