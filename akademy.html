Fellowship I — Fall 2026
Documentary Fellowship
For emerging documentary photographers and filmmakers working on long-form projects rooted in community, accountability, or social consequence. Fellows receive sustained mentorship from Jon Lowenstein and invited practitioners, access to the Artefakt archive, and a structured framework for developing their projects over twelve months.

This is not a workshop series. It is a committed working relationship. Fellows arrive with a project in progress and leave with it materially advanced — photographically, editorially, and ethically. Fellows present their work publicly at the close of the fellowship year as part of the Bearing Witness series.
Open Call — Summer 2026
Fellowship II — Spring/Summer 2027
Sustainability Documentary/Arts Fellowship
For practitioners and organizers working at the intersection of documentary practice, art, and material sustainability — the ethical disposal and redistribution of technology, the circular economies of creative production, and the environmental responsibilities of institutions that make culture.

Fellows work alongside Artefakt's Sustain program, developing frameworks that other organizations can adopt. The Sustainability Fellowship reflects a core conviction: the organizations building culture must themselves be accountable to the world they're documenting.

Fellowship Structure — Both Programs
Eligibility
Open to emerging and mid-career practitioners in Calgary and Chicago. No formal education requirement. Work and commitment are the criteria.
Selection
Applications reviewed by Jon Lowenstein and a rotating panel of practicing documentary artists and educators. Selection is based on project strength, community relationship, and demonstrated seriousness of purpose.
What Fellows Receive
Mentorship, access to Artefakt archive and studio resources, participation in all Akademy programming, and public presentation at year's end. Stipend details confirmed with each cohort's open call.
Fellow Obligations
Monthly cohort sessions, a mid-term project review, and contribution to one Akademy public event. The fellowship is a commitment, not a credential.
How to Apply
Full application guidelines published with each open call. Write to jon@artefakt.foundation to register interest and receive notification when applications open.

Bearing Witness — Lecture & Exhibition Series Akademy 05 / 10
2026–27 Season

Four presentations. One year.

Bearing Witness is Artefakt's annual lecture and exhibition series — internationally significant documentary practitioners brought to Calgary to teach, exhibit, and speak in public. Programming is built from Jon Lowenstein's working relationships. All public lectures are free and open to the public.

01
Kiana Hayeri
Iranian-Canadian documentary photographer whose practice has been rooted in Afghanistan for more than a decade. Her sustained documentation of Afghan women and girls — through the US withdrawal and into Taliban resurgence — is among the most significant bodies of documentary work produced in recent years. Published in The New York Times, National Geographic, and Time.
Sept / Oct 2026
02
Amber Bracken
One of Canada's most significant working documentary photographers. Her coverage of the Wet'suwet'en pipeline resistance — made on the frontlines alongside land defenders at Gidimt'en checkpoint — earned the World Press Photo of the Year in 2022. Presentation aligned with the Exposure Photography Festival.
Feb 2027
03
Kitra Cahana
Photographer and filmmaker whose work explores spirituality, altered states of consciousness, and communities living outside mainstream structures. Her photographs of Hasidic community life — including intimate work made with her father, Rabbi Mordecai Cahana, whose family roots are in Calgary — represent documentary practice built from belonging rather than observation.
May 2027
04
Carlos Javier Ortiz + Darcy Padilla — Joint Exhibition
The closing presentation pairs two photographers united by a structural commitment: they stayed. Ortiz spent more than a decade documenting the human cost of gun violence in Chicago's communities of colour — We All We Got. Padilla spent eighteen years in relationship with one woman and her family living in poverty in San Francisco — Family Love. Shown together, the two bodies of work make an argument that exceeds either individually.
Sept / Oct 2027

All presentations include a public lecture free and open to the public. Associated practitioner workshops offered where artist availability permits. For press, partnership, and venue inquiries contact jon@artefakt.foundation.

Workshops Akademy 07 / 10
All Akademy Workshops

Seven workshops. One standard.

Taught at 4525 Monterey Ave NW, Calgary and, when programming warrants, in Chicago. Open to the public. Fellows receive priority registration.

01
Bearing Witness: The Ethics of Documentary Photography
A two-day intensive on the foundational ethical questions of documentary practice. When do you photograph? When do you stop? What do you owe the people you photograph? Taught by Jon Lowenstein, drawing on over twenty-five years of embedded practice on Chicago's South Side and across the Americas.
Witness
02
The Long Project: Building a Documentary Body of Work
For photographers and filmmakers developing sustained, multi-year projects rooted in community trust. Covers project structure, editorial logic, community relationship-building, and the discipline required to stay in one place long enough to see the truth. Includes individual project critique sessions.
Witness
03
The Edit: Sequencing, Narrative, and the Decisive Selection
A focused workshop on the craft of editing — how a body of work becomes a story, how sequence creates meaning, and how the hardest decision in documentary is knowing which image not to use. Participants bring 200 images from a single project. They leave with 25.
Witness
04
Publishing and Distribution: Getting the Work Into the World
A practical workshop covering editorial pitching, book development, exhibition strategies, grant applications, and the digital platforms that sustain independent documentary practice. No gatekeeping mythology — the actual mechanics of how work finds its audience.
Akademy
05
Archive as Practice: Preservation, Access, and the Permanent Record
On the obligations documentary practitioners have to their own archives — and to the communities they've photographed. Covers physical and digital preservation, metadata, licensing, and how to think about who owns the image once the moment has passed.
Preserve
06
Sustaining the Practice: Economic Models for Independent Documentary Work
A direct conversation about money, sustainability, and independence. How do you fund long-term work without compromising it? What are the real economics of a documentary practice — grants, editorial, licensing, workshops, residencies? Taught with radical transparency about what actually works.
Sustain
07
Masterclass Series: Visiting Practitioners
Single-session masterclasses tied to the Bearing Witness lecture and exhibition series. Each visiting artist offers a two-hour public masterclass in addition to their lecture. Open to the public with priority registration for Akademy fellows and workshop alumni.

How the Akademy Teaches Akademy 09 / 10
Four Principles

What the Akademy holds.

01
Practice precedes theory. Every Akademy session begins with work on the table — photographs, sequences, projects in progress. We discuss ideas through the images, not instead of them.
02
The community relationship comes first. Documentary work that is not grounded in genuine relationship with its subjects is not documentary work. The Akademy teaches this distinction and holds it.
03
Failure is curriculum. The photographs that didn't work, the access that fell through, the story that turned out to be different — these are the lessons. We teach from what happened, not from what should have.
04
Sustainability is not a compromise. The Akademy includes economic education because independence requires it. A practice that cannot sustain itself cannot bear witness over time. This is craft, not concession.
