Pillar 03 / 04 Preserve The Archive

What the photograph keeps.

Preserve is the permanent Artefakt Archive — a publicly accessible record of documentary photography and film made over decades in Chicago, Calgary, across the Americas, and beyond. The archive is both a reference for researchers and a working collection: all new accessions are catalogued, held with consent, and made accessible to researchers for the communities who appear in it.

Accession · Housing & Displacement

Work that took years.

The archive holds long-form projects that no editorial deadline would have allowed. Years of return visits, of earned access, of staying when the news cycle moved on. These are the photographs that only exist because someone stayed.

PRV · 02 Community Trust
Accession · Community Trust

Held in trust.

All new accessions to the archive are attached to a consent record. The communities and individuals photographed have say in how their images are used, circulated, and licensed. This is documentary practice that doesn't end when the shutter closes — it continues for as long as the photograph exists.

PRV · 03 Still Life · Landscape
Accession · Working Collection

Not only people.

Documentary is a discipline of attention. The archive holds landscapes, still lifes, objects left behind, the weather of a place. These frames are not decoration — they carry the texture that makes the people-pictures legible. They belong in the record.

PRV · 04 Cities in Transition
Accession · Place

Cities get rewritten.

The archive watches Calgary and Chicago both — two cities on very different trajectories, bound together by Jon's practice and by the working project If You Lived Here You'd Be Home By Now. The archive is the only place where both cities are held in one record, frame by frame, block by block.

Construction · Calgary · 2024

PRV · 05 Restricted Access
Accession · International

What the world rarely shows.

The archive also holds reportage from places that don't let photographers in easily, or at all. These accessions are held with extra care — with respect to the people who appear in them and the conditions under which the work was made. They are part of the record precisely because they are rare.

North Korea · 2015
