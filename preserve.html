Preserve
The Archive

What the photograph keeps.

Preserve is the permanent Artefakt Archive — a publicly accessible record of documentary photography and film made over three decades in Chicago, Calgary, across the Americas, and beyond. Every frame is accessioned, catalogued, and held in trust for the communities who appear in it. The archive is both a reference for researchers and a working collection — because the record is not finished.

Preserve · Archive · Holdings
Geography
Chicago (South Side), Calgary (Treaty 7 Territory), Mexico, Central America, and ongoing work across the Americas.
Time span
1995 – present. Three decades of documentary practice, from early South Side work through Shadow Lives USA and current Calgary projects.
Access
Publicly accessible by appointment. Researchers, curators, educators, and community members are all welcome. Contact jon@artefakt.foundation to arrange access.
Licensing
Print licensing and press inquiries handled directly. Revenue from licensing flows back to foundation programming.
Consent
Every frame in the archive is accessioned with the knowledge and consent of those who appear in it, to the extent that this can be obtained. Where consent cannot be confirmed, the frame is not made publicly available.
