Practice

Long-form,
across decades.

Jon Lowenstein is a documentary photographer and filmmaker whose work sits at the intersection of long-form documentary, oral history, and cross-border storytelling. For over twenty-five years he has worked on the South Side of Chicago, and more recently in Calgary, making photographs and films that follow people and places across years rather than news cycles.

His long-term projects include Shadow Lives USA, a decade-plus documentation of migration from Central America and Mexico; sustained coverage of housing, displacement, and community life on the South Side; and If You Lived Here You'd Be Home By Now, a multidisciplinary project involving the cross-border exchange of two demolition-slated houses — one in Calgary, one in Chicago — reconstructed as working studios and community spaces.

Recognition

Guggenheim Fellow, National Geographic Explorer, TED Senior Fellow, founding member of NOOR Images. Work published, exhibited, and collected internationally.

Legal Entity

Alberta Society,
est. 2026.

The Artefakt Foundation is incorporated as an Alberta Society. The organization operates from 4525 Monterey Avenue NW in Calgary, with programming and archival work also carried out in Chicago through partner institutions and Jon Lowenstein Studio.

Artefakt Foundation and Jon Lowenstein Studio are separate legal entities with separate visual identities, separate accounts, and separate financial operations. The Studio is Jon's commercial practice; the Foundation is the institution. They are connected by shared work and shared archive, not by shared money.
