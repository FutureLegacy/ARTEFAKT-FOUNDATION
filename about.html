Artefakt Foundation

Documentary, from inside the practice.

Artefakt is an independent documentary photography, film, and arts organization based in Calgary (Treaty 7 Territory) and Chicago. Built from inside documentary practice rather than alongside it, Artefakt supports long-form work, teaches the craft to the next practitioners, preserves the record in a permanent public archive, and sustains itself through an ethical materials-reuse operation.

Biography Jon Lowenstein 04 / 07

Over twenty-five years, in two cities.

Jon Lowenstein is a documentary photographer and filmmaker whose work sits at the intersection of long-form documentary, oral history, and cross-border storytelling.

Practice

For over twenty-five years he has worked on the South Side of Chicago, and more recently in Calgary, making photographs and films that follow people and places across years rather than news cycles.

His long-term projects include Shadow Lives USA, a decade-plus documentation of migration from Central America and Mexico to the United States; sustained coverage of housing, displacement, and community life on the South Side; and If You Lived Here You'd Be Home By Now, a current multidisciplinary project involving the cross-border exchange of two demolition-slated houses — one in Calgary, one in Chicago.

Recognition

Jon is a Guggenheim Fellow, a National Geographic Explorer, and a TED Senior Fellow. He is a founding member of the NOOR Images photo collective. His work has been published, exhibited, and collected internationally, and has been recognized with grants and honors from the Alicia Patterson Foundation, the Aaron Siskind Foundation, the Open Society Foundations' Documentary Photography Project, and others.

Organization Artefakt Foundation 05 / 07

Built from inside the practice.

Legal entity

The Artefakt Foundation is incorporated as an Alberta Society. The organization operates from 4525 Monterey Avenue NW in Calgary, with programming and archival work also carried out in Chicago.

Artefakt Foundation and Jon Lowenstein Studio are separate legal entities with separate visual identities, separate accounts, and separate financial operations. The Studio is Jon's commercial practice; the Foundation is the institution. They are connected by shared work and shared archive, not by shared money.

Four pillars

Witness — Long-form documentary practice grounded in community trust and ethical rigor.

Akademy — Fellowship for emerging practitioners and Akademy of workshops and masterclasses taught by working documentarians.

Preserve — A permanent, publicly accessible archive of the work and its sources.

Sustain — An ethical materials-reuse operation whose revenue funds the programming. The origin story is the mission: nothing of value should be thrown away.

Artefakt · Why

Most arts organizations are built alongside practice. Artefakt is built from inside it — from what thirty years of documentary photography actually teaches.
