01 — Witness

Long-form work.
Sustained attention.

Artefakt exists to produce and support documentary work built on time — months and years in place, not days. Work where the photographer stayed long enough for the place to get into the photographs.

From Chicago's South Side to Ukraine, from North Korea to the communities forming around our Calgary gallery — witness is not a style. It is a commitment.

02 — Connect

Fellowship.
Academy. Platform.

The Artefakt Fellowship supports emerging documentary practitioners in Calgary and Chicago — photographers, filmmakers, and visual artists committed to long-form community work.

The Academy brings visiting practitioners into direct conversation with the next generation through the Bearing Witness lecture series, masterclasses, and workshops.

03 — Legacy

Archive.
Education. Memory.

A permanent, publicly accessible archive of documentary work — photographs, film, and collected materials — treated as infrastructure rather than inventory.

The archive is not nostalgia. It is an operating system for what comes next. Every image acquired, catalogued, and made available to researchers, educators, and institutions.

Explore the archive
04 — Sustain

Nothing of value
gets thrown away.

Artefakt runs an ethical materials reuse and e-cycling operation. Discarded electronics, recovered equipment, and surplus materials are repaired, resold, or redistributed — generating revenue that funds the other three pillars.

Material stewardship mirrors documentary integrity. What endures, endures for a reason.

05 — About

Built from
inside the work.

Artefakt Foundation is a documentary photography, film, and arts institution operating across Canada and the United States. Based at 4525 Monterey Ave NW, Calgary, Treaty 7 Territory.

Founded by Jon Lowenstein, whose practice spans more than twenty years of sustained documentary work in Chicago's South Side and across the world. Artefakt is built from inside that practice — not alongside it.

An Alberta Society. Independent. Supported by people who believe the photograph is evidence.
