01 — the construction logic

The alphabet is built from two shapes: the circle and the vertical. Nothing else.

Strokes are continuous and monolinear — constant 14 unit weight across the entire alphabet. Every curve is a pure circular arc. Every terminal is cut square at the baseline.

This is geometric construction in the lineage of Bayer's Universal and the Bauhaus lowercase alphabets. Calm, confident, nothing decorative. The word is the idea.

design principles

Geometric. Calm. The letters are made of the two most elemental shapes — a circle and a line. That restraint is the voice.

The 'f' is safelight red: the one accent, the only colour interruption in the wordmark. Everywhere else, the type is confident and whole.
