Pillar 03 / 04 — Legacy — The Archive

What the
photograph keeps.

A publicly accessible record of documentary photography and film made over three decades in Chicago, Calgary, across the Americas, and beyond. Every frame is accessioned, catalogued, and held in trust for the communities who appear in it.

Accession · Housing & Displacement

Work that
took years.

The archive holds long-form projects that no editorial deadline would have allowed. Years of return visits, of earned access, of staying when the news cycle moved on.

LGY · 02 — Community Trust
Tent City · Chicago · 2019

Consent · On File

Held in trust.

Every accession in the archive is attached to a consent record. The communities and individuals photographed have say in how their images are used, circulated, and licensed. This is documentary practice that doesn't end when the shutter closes — it continues for as long as the photograph exists.

LGY · 03
Still Life · Landscape
Accession · Working Collection

Not only people.

Documentary is a discipline of attention. The archive holds landscapes, still lifes, objects left behind, the weather of a place. These frames carry the texture that makes the people-pictures legible.

LGY · 04 — Cities in Transition
Construction · Calgary · 2024

Calgary · Chicago

Cities get rewritten.

The archive watches Calgary and Chicago both — two cities on very different trajectories, bound together by Jon's practice and by the working project If You Lived Here You'd Be Home By Now.

The archive is the only place where both cities are held in one record, frame by frame, block by block.
